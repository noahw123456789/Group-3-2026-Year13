Adventure starts the moment you land

Activities in Queenstown

From bungy jumps over glacial rivers to jet boats tearing through canyon walls, Queenstown is built for the ones who want more. Every corner of this place dares you to go further — and somehow, you always do.

Explore Activities
Thrill & Adventure
AJ Hackett Bungy Jump
Adrenaline Heights

Leap from the original Kawarau Bridge — 43 metres above a glacial river — where the modern bungy craze was born in 1988. A pilgrimage for thrill-seekers, with optional water-touch for the brave.

View Details
Shotover Jet
Adrenaline Water

Hold on tight as you blast through the narrow Shotover River canyons at 85 km/h, inches from sheer rock walls. The signature 360° spins are equal parts terrifying and unforgettable.

View Details
Cardrona Alpine Resort
Winter Heights

One of New Zealand's premier ski fields, with wide-open groomed runs for families and steep terrain for experts. Come winter, Cardrona transforms the Crown Range into a powder-covered playground.

View Details
Scenery & Leisure
Skyline Gondola & Luge
Views Nature

Ride the Southern Hemisphere's steepest gondola for panoramic views over Lake Wakatipu and the Remarkables. Once at the top, try the exhilarating luge track or simply linger over the view with a meal at the summit restaurant.

View Details
Milford Sound Day Tour
Scenic Nature

Journey through Fiordland National Park to the world-famous Milford Sound — a UNESCO World Heritage site carved by ancient glaciers. Cruise beneath towering waterfalls and spot seals, dolphins, and rare Fiordland penguins.

View Details
Central Otago Wine Trail
Wine Food

Wander through the world's southernmost wine region, where dramatic schist-rock landscapes frame some of New Zealand's finest Pinot Noir. Visit boutique cellar doors in Gibbston Valley, just 20 minutes from Queenstown.

View Details
Explore & Discover
Arrowtown Heritage Walk
Free Nature

Wander the tree-lined streets of one of New Zealand's best-preserved gold rush towns, just 20 minutes from Queenstown. Arrowtown's 1860s cottages, Chinese settlement ruins, and autumn foliage make it one of the most photogenic spots in the entire region — and it costs nothing to explore.

View Details
TSS Earnslaw Steamship Cruise
Historic Nature

Step aboard the last remaining coal-fired passenger steamship in the Southern Hemisphere, which has been sailing Lake Wakatipu since 1912. The 1.5-hour cruise takes you across the glacial lake to Walter Peak High Country Farm, where you can watch sheepdogs work and warm up by the fire.

View Details
Queenstown Market
Local Food

Every Thursday night, the Queenstown waterfront comes alive with over 50 local stalls serving street food from around the world, live music, and handmade crafts. It's the best place to eat like a local, meet fellow travellers, and experience the laid-back side of this otherwise adrenaline-charged town.
