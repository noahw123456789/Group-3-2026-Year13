Start planning your trip to Queenstown

Queenstown is located in the southern lakes region of the South Island of New Zealand. Surrounded by dramatic mountains and the crystal-clear Lake Whakatipu, Queenstown is renowned for its spectacular scenery, four distinct seasons and range of incredible activities and attractions.

To help you craft the perfect trip, we've pulled together everything you need — from travel tips and itinerary ideas, to essential how-to's and local services.

Getting to Queenstown

How will you get here?

Flying into Queenstown

Queenstown Airport (ZQN) is serviced by direct flights from Auckland, Wellington, Christchurch, Sydney, Melbourne and Brisbane. It is one of New Zealand's busiest airports and the gateway to the southern lakes region.

Several airlines operate regular routes into Queenstown, making it easy to connect from major hubs across New Zealand and Australia.

More on getting here →

Driving to Queenstown

Queenstown is well connected by road. From Christchurch it's approximately a 4.5-hour drive via State Highway 8 through the stunning Mackenzie Basin. From Dunedin it's around 3 hours via SH6.

The drive itself is part of the experience — passing through dramatic mountain passes, wide-open plains and stunning lake views along the way.

FAQs

With four distinct seasons, it's always an ideal time to explore Queenstown. Summer (December–February) brings long warm days with temperatures of 20–30°C. Autumn (March–May) brings dramatic gold and red foliage. Winter (June–August) transforms the region into a ski destination, and Spring (September–November) is ideal for doing a bit of everything.

5–7 days is recommended to get a proper feel for Queenstown, meet the locals, and venture beyond the usual hotspots. There is so much to do that 2–3 days simply isn't enough time to experience it all.

Queenstown is commonly known as the adventure capital of the world. It's the birthplace of commercial bungy, and home to the world's highest cliff jump, world's steepest zipline, and one of the world's largest giant swings.

The best things to do depend on your interests, travel style and time of year. Whether it's adventure, sightseeing, food and wine, or relaxation — there's something for everyone. We recommend at least a week to experience Queenstown like a local.
