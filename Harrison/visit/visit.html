Places to visit page

Below this text are a few highlights of Queenstown that majority of people say are must go places. A brief description of each place will be given however, further details will be given in the corresponding pages.

The Remarkables
Adrenaline Heights

A dramatic mountain range 20km from Queenstown. In winter it operates as a ski field with 220 hectares of terrain. In summer, it offers walking tracks and rock climbing. Free to visit outside the ski season.

View Details
Queenstown Arts Centre
Historic

Located in the town centre, this community arts hub features local and national artists. Regular evening events, life drawing classes, and a gallery shop. Entry is free for most exhibitions.

View Details
Blue Kanu
Food & Drink

Healthy, fresh and exotic Asian cuisine with a Pacific influence. Reservations are needed and the restaurant is opened from 4pm-10pm. The restaurant is Vegetarian friendly, Vegan options.

View Details
Skyline Bike Park
Adrenaline Nature

Accessible via the Skyline Gondola, this bike park has over 30km of trails across all skill levels. Gondola and bike hire packages available. Open daily in summer. Price from NZD $75.

View Details
Gibbston Valley Winery
Food & Drink Wine

Located in the Gibbston Valley, 35 minutes from Queenstown. Famous for its wine cave carved into schist rock. Tasting sessions from NZD $20. Restaurant on site. Open daily 10am–5pm.

View Details
Lake Wakatipu
Nature Scenic

The third-largest lake in NZ, stretching 80km. The TSS Earnslaw steamship runs scenic cruises from Queenstown Bay. The Queenstown Trail follows the shoreline for cyclists and walkers. Access is free.
